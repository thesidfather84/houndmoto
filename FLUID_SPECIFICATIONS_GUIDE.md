# HoundMoto Fluid Specifications Guide

## ⚠️ IMPORTANT DISCLAIMER

**ALWAYS verify fluid specifications in your vehicle's owner's manual before adding ANY fluid.**

**Wrong fluid can cause:**
- Engine damage
- Transmission failure
- Brake system failure
- Power steering damage
- Overheating
- Complete vehicle breakdown

**When in doubt, consult your owner's manual or a qualified mechanic.**

---

## How to Find Your Vehicle's Fluids

### Method 1: Owner's Manual
- Fastest and most accurate
- Usually in maintenance section
- Shows exact capacities and types
- Location: In your glove box

### Method 2: Under the Hood
- Oil cap: Says "5W-30" or similar
- Coolant cap: Usually says "COOLANT"
- Transmission: Dipstick label
- Brake fluid: Says "BRAKE FLUID"
- Power steering: Reservoir cap label

### Method 3: Vehicle Door Jamb
- Inside driver's side door
- Shows tire pressure
- May show oil type

### Method 4: Manufacturer Website
- Enter your VIN
- Get official specifications
- Most reliable source

## 1. ENGINE OIL

### What It Does
- Lubricates engine parts
- Prevents wear
- Cools engine components
- Removes contaminants

### Common Grades
- **0W-20**: Modern Toyota, Honda, Ford (thinner, better cold flow)
- **0W-30**: Some newer engines
- **5W-20**: Some Ford, Toyota
- **5W-30**: Nissan, Chevrolet, older vehicles
- **5W-40**: Diesel engines
- **10W-30**: Older vehicles, diesel
- **10W-40**: Older vehicles, hot climates

### Typical Capacities
- **Sedans**: 3.5-4.5 quarts
- **SUVs**: 5-6 quarts
- **Trucks**: 5-7 quarts
- **Diesel**: 7-10 quarts

### Change Interval
- **Modern (2010+)**: 7,500-10,000 miles
- **Older (2000-2009)**: 3,000-5,000 miles
- **Synthetic**: 10,000-15,000 miles
- **Older with synthetic**: Every 5,000 miles

### Synthetic vs Conventional
| Type | Cost | Interval | Life |
|------|------|----------|------|
| Conventional | $25-40 | 3,000-5,000 mi | Budget option |
| Blend | $40-60 | 5,000-7,500 mi | Middle ground |
| Synthetic | $60-100 | 10,000-15,000 mi | Premium, lasts longer |

### If You Use Wrong Grade:
- Too thin (lower number): Excess wear, low pressure
- Too thick (higher number): Hard cold starts, excessive pressure

## 2. COOLANT

### What It Does
- Prevents freezing (-34°F)
- Prevents boiling (above 220°F)
- Protects against rust
- Lubricates water pump

### Color Guide
| Color | Type | Vehicle Examples |
|-------|------|------------------|
| Green | IAT (older) | Pre-2000 vehicles |
| Orange | OAT (modern) | Toyota, Honda 2005+ |
| Pink/Red | HOAT | Some Ford, Chrysler |
| Blue | OAT | Some GM, Nissan |
| Yellow | OAT | Some Hyundai, Kia |

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: Never Mix Coolants
**Mixing colors can:**
- Damage water pump
- Clog cooling system
- Cause overheating
- Void warranty

### Typical Capacities
- **Sedans**: 4-6 quarts
- **SUVs**: 6-8 quarts
- **Trucks**: 7-10 quarts

### Concentration
- **50/50 Mix**: Coolant + Distilled Water
- **Pre-mixed**: Already diluted
- **Concentrate**: Requires dilution

### Change Interval
- **Green (IAT)**: Every 30,000 miles
- **Orange/Pink (OAT/HOAT)**: Every 100,000 miles
- **Some newer**: Every 10 years or 150,000 miles
- **Flush & fill**: Follow manufacturer schedule

### Brands by Manufacturer
| Manufacturer | Coolant |
|---|---|
| Toyota | Toyota Coolant, ZEREX G05 |
| Honda | Honda HAS Coolant |
| Nissan | Nissan Engine Coolant |
| Ford | Motorcraft Coolant |
| Chevrolet | GM Dex-Cool |
| Dodge | Mopar Coolant |
| Jeep | Mopar Coolant |

## 3. TRANSMISSION FLUID

### What It Does
- Lubrication
- Cooling (heat dissipation)
- Hydraulic pressure
- Clutch engagement
- Prevents wear

### Automatic Transmission (ATF)
| Type | Usage |
|------|-------|
| ATF IV | Toyota, Lexus |
| DW-1 | Honda, Acura |
| Mercon V | Ford, Lincoln, Mercury |
| Dex-Merc | GM, Cadillac |
| ATF+4 | Chrysler, Dodge, Jeep |
| NS-2 | Nissan, Infiniti |
| CVT Fluid | Subaru, Nissan CVTs |

### Manual Transmission
| Type | Usage |
|------|-------|
| 75W-90 | Most vehicles |
| 75W-85 | Some Honda |
| 80W-90 | Some trucks |

### Typical Capacities
- **Automatic**: 4-8 quarts (partial fill)
- **Manual**: 2-3 quarts
- **CVT**: 2-4 quarts

### Change Interval
- **Automatic**: Every 30,000-100,000 miles (varies greatly)
- **Manual**: Every 30,000-60,000 miles or as needed
- **CVT**: Every 30,000 miles or per manufacturer

### ⚠️ CRITICAL: Wrong Fluid Causes
- Transmission slipping
- Harsh shifts
- Overheating
- Complete transmission failure
- $2,000-4,000 repair cost

## 4. BRAKE FLUID

### What It Does
- Hydraulic pressure transmission
- Compresses under pressure
- Activates brake calipers/cylinders
- Absorbs moisture

### Types
| Type | Boiling Point | Usage |
|------|---|---|
| DOT 3 | 401°F | Most vehicles pre-2000 |
| DOT 4 | 446°F | Modern vehicles |
| DOT 5 | 500°F | Some specialty vehicles |
| DOT 5.1 | 500°F | Performance, racing |

### ⚠️ Brake fluid absorbs water
- Needs replacement every 2 years
- Water lowers boiling point
- Can cause brake failure
- Hygroscopic (pulls moisture from air)

### Typical Capacity
- **Most vehicles**: 0.5-1.5 quarts
- **Check master cylinder** to see current level

### Change Interval
- **Every 2 years minimum**
- **Or if contaminated**
- **Don't go longer than 3 years**

### If Level Drops
- You have a leak
- Have brakes inspected immediately
- Don't continue driving

### Never Mix Brake Fluids
- DOT 3 + DOT 4 = okay (DOT 4 is upgrade)
- DOT 3 + DOT 5 = NO (damages seals)
- DOT 4 + DOT 5 = NO (incompatible)

## 5. POWER STEERING FLUID

### What It Does
- Hydraulic pressure
- Makes steering easier
- Lubricates pump and hoses
- Prevents corrosion

### Common Types
| Manufacturer | Fluid |
|---|---|
| Toyota | Toyota PSF, Dexron III |
| Honda | Honda PS Fluid |
| Ford | Motorcraft Fluid |
| Nissan | Nissan PSF |
| Chevy/GM | Dexron III |
| Chrysler | Mopar ATF+4 |

### Typical Capacity
- **Most vehicles**: 1-2 quarts
- **Check reservoir** to see current level

### Change Interval
- **As needed** (usually every 50,000-100,000 miles)
- **Or if discolored/dark**
- **No strict schedule** (unlike oil)

### Signs of Low Fluid
- Stiff steering wheel
- Squealing noise from pump
- Jerky steering
- Difficulty turning

### ⚠️ Leaks Need Repair
- Don't just top off repeatedly
- Find the source of leak
- Usually hoses or pump seals

## 6. FUEL TYPE

### Gasoline Octane Ratings
| Octane | Price | Best For |
|--------|-------|----------|
| 87 | Cheapest | Regular engines |
| 89 | Middle | Most vehicles |
| 91 | Premium | High-compression engines |
| 93 | Premium | Performance/luxury |

### How to Know Your Car's Requirement
- **Owner's manual** - Most reliable
- **Fuel door label** - Usually shows minimum
- **Below gas cap** - Sometimes indicated
- **Dashboard** - Some cars show on startup

### What Happens if Wrong Octane
- **Too low**: Knocking/pinging, reduced power, poor mileage
- **Too high**: Wastes money (car doesn't need it)
- **One tank wrong**: Usually okay, mix with correct next fill

### Regular vs Premium: When to Use
- **Use regular (87)** unless manual says otherwise
- **Don't use premium to "clean" engine** (doesn't work)
- **Premium helps high-compression engines run smooth**
- **Performance vehicles need premium** (consult manual)

### Typical Fuel Capacity
- **Sedans**: 12-16 gallons
- **SUVs**: 18-25 gallons
- **Trucks**: 20-35 gallons
- **Check fuel door** for exact amount

### E10 vs E15
- **E10**: 10% ethanol (safe for all vehicles post-1980)
- **E15**: 15% ethanol (check manual - not all cars okay)
- **E85**: 85% ethanol (flex-fuel vehicles only)

## 7. TIRE PRESSURE

### Why It Matters
- Safety
- Fuel economy
- Tire life
- Handling

### How to Find Correct Pressure
- **Driver's door jamb**: Most reliable
- **Owner's manual**: Also in here
- **Tire sidewall**: Shows MAXIMUM, not recommended
- **Never use tire pressure on door jamb** - that's the cap max, not recommended

### Typical Pressures
- **Sedans**: 30-35 PSI
- **SUVs**: 32-38 PSI
- **Trucks (empty)**: 32-40 PSI
- **Trucks (loaded)**: 38-50 PSI

### When to Check
- **Monthly** when cold (before driving)
- **Before long trips**
- **When temperature drops** (pressure drops 1 PSI per 10°F)
- **If tire looks low**

### Nitrogen vs Air
- **Nitrogen**: Slight advantages (slower leaks)
- **Regular air**: Fine for most drivers
- **Usually not worth the cost difference**

### Tire Grades (On Sidewall)
| Rating | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| 100 | Load rating |
| AA | Traction grade (AA, A, B, C) |
| A | Temperature grade (A, B, C) |

## 8. WINDSHIELD WASHER FLUID

### What It Does
- Cleans windshield
- Helps wipers work
- Improves visibility

### Types
| Type | Temperature |
|------|---|
| Summer | Down to 32°F |
| Winter | Down to -20°F |
| Extreme | Down to -40°F |

### Typical Capacity
- **Most vehicles**: 1-2 gallons
- **Check by** looking at reservoir under hood

### Cost
- **Very cheap**: $3-8 per gallon
- **Buy pre-filled** or refill yourself

### What Washer to Buy
- **All-weather** works for most people
- **Winter formula** if temperature drops below freezing
- **Avoid brands that freeze** in cold climates

## 9. DIFFERENTIAL FLUID (Trucks/AWD)

### What It Does
- Lubricates gears
- Reduces friction
- Prevents wear

### Types
- **80W-90**: Most common
- **75W-90**: Some modern vehicles
- **75W-85**: Limited slip (with additive)

### Typical Capacity
- **2.5-5 quarts** depending on vehicle

### Change Interval
- **Every 30,000-50,000 miles** (varies)
- **More often if towing or severe driving**
- **Check manual** for your vehicle

### How to Check
- **Fill plug** under vehicle
- **Smell (dark/burnt smell = needs change)**
- **Temperature check** near fill plug

---

## 10. TRANSFER CASE FLUID (4WD/AWD Only)

### What It Does
- Lubricates transfer case gears
- Powers 4WD engagement
- Prevents wear

### Type
- **ATF Dexron**: Most vehicles
- **Synthetic**: Some modern vehicles

### Typical Capacity
- **1-3 quarts**

### Change Interval
- **Every 30,000-50,000 miles** (if 4WD used frequently)
- **Check manual** for specific vehicle

## FLUID SHOPPING TIPS

### Where to Buy
- AutoZone
- O'Reilly Auto Parts
- RockAuto
- Amazon
- Walmart

### How to Buy
1. **Know your car's year, make, model**
2. **Know what fluid you need** (oil, coolant, etc.)

4. **Buy the right quantity** (don't overfill)

## WHEN TO REPLACE FLUIDS

### Oil Change
- **Frequency**: Every 3,000-15,000 miles (per manual)
- **Signs**: Dark color, low dipstick
- **DIY Cost**: $30-60
- **Shop Cost**: $50-200

### Coolant Flush
- **Frequency**: Every 30,000-100,000 miles (per manual)
- **Signs**: Dark/discolored, low level
- **DIY Cost**: $50-100
- **Shop Cost**: $150-300

### Transmission Fluid
- **Frequency**: Every 30,000-100,000 miles (per manual)
- **Signs**: Dark/burnt smell, shifting issues
- **DIY Cost**: Difficult (usually shop)
- **Shop Cost**: $150-400

### Brake Fluid
- **Frequency**: Every 2 years
- **Signs**: Spongey pedal, low level
- **DIY Cost**: $20-50
- **Shop Cost**: $100-300

### Power Steering Fluid
- **Frequency**: As needed (50,000-100,000 miles)
- **Signs**: Stiff steering, squealing, low level
- **DIY Cost**: $20-40
- **Shop Cost**: $100-250

- ❌ Drive with low brake fluid
- ❌ Use wrong transmission fluid
- ❌ Mix coolant colors
- ❌ Ignore low oil pressure light
- ❌ Fill radiator when hot

- ❌ Overfill any fluid

### Always:
- ✅ Check manual for specifications
- ✅ Use the right fluid the first time

- ✅ Let engine cool before checking oil
- ✅ Follow change intervals
